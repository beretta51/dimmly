# The Best Simple Todo App for iPhone With No Subscription
If you've gone looking for a simple todo app for iPhone with no subscription lately, you already know the problem: there's no such thing as simple anymore. Every app wants your email address, a monthly fee, and five minutes of onboarding before you can write down "buy milk." Somewhere between the Kanban boards and the AI-powered priority matrices, the humble to-do list got buried.
This post is for the people who just want to get things out of their head and into a list — quickly, privately, and without paying $8 a month forever.
## Why Most To-Do Apps Have Gotten Too Complicated
The productivity app market has drifted toward power users. That's fine if you're managing a product roadmap, but most of us aren't. Most of us have groceries to buy, emails to send, and a few things we're trying to do every day — like drink more water or go for a walk.
The pain points are consistent:
**Too many taps to add a task.** If creating a to-do requires opening a form, picking a list, setting a due date, and choosing a priority level, the friction alone defeats the purpose. By the time you've done all that, you've already forgotten what you were trying to remember.
**Subscriptions for basic features.** It's hard to justify $5–$10 a month for an app that replaces a piece of paper. And yet that's the standard pricing model now, even for apps with no team features and nothing that actually requires server infrastructure.
**Separate apps for tasks and habits.** Plenty of people end up with a task manager in one app and a habit tracker in another, then a third app to feel bad about not using the first two.
**Your data lives on someone else's server.** Account-required apps mean your lists are tied to a company's continued existence, their privacy policy, and their ability to keep the lights on.
## What a Gesture-First Approach Actually Feels Like
There's a different philosophy worth considering: what if the app got out of the way entirely?
**Dimmly** is a task manager for iPhone built around gestures instead of menus. Pull down to create a task. Swipe to complete it. Drag to reorder. That's genuinely most of what you need to do in a to-do app, and here it happens without opening a single form or navigating a single menu.
It combines tasks and habits in one list without making habits the centerpiece. You can mark something as a habit and it'll track how many times you've done it — but it lives right alongside your regular tasks rather than in a separate section that feels like a different app. The streak system counts total daily completions rather than consecutive days, which turns out to be more motivating for most people. Missing one day doesn't erase your progress.
Data stays on your device. No account required. Nothing synced to a server you don't control.

But if what you actually need is a fast, frictionless place to capture tasks and track a few daily habits — and you'd rather not pay a subscription or hand over your data to do it — it's worth a look.
