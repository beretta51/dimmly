# Dimmly vs Todoist: Which Should You Choose in 2025?
If you're weighing Dimmly against Todoist, you're probably asking a straightforward question: do I need a powerful productivity platform, or do I just want to get things done without the friction? Both apps help you manage tasks, but they're built on very different philosophies.
## What Is Dimmly?
Dimmly is a gesture-first task manager and habit tracker for iPhone. There are no forms to fill out, no menus to dig through. You pull down to create a task, swipe to complete it, and drag to reorder. It also tracks habits, but it approaches them differently than most apps — streaks count total daily repetitions rather than consecutive days, which is a more forgiving and arguably more honest measure of consistency. Dimmly costs $6.99 once, requires no account, and keeps your data on your device.
## What Is Todoist?
Todoist is one of the most fully-featured task managers available. It supports projects, sub-tasks, labels, filters, priority levels, recurring due dates, team collaboration, calendar integrations, and more. It's available on virtually every platform and syncs seamlessly across all of them. The free tier is functional, but most of its best features sit behind a subscription that runs around $48 per year.
## Feature Comparison
| Feature | Dimmly | Todoist |
|---|---|---|  
| Platform | iPhone only | iOS, Android, Web, Mac, Windows |
| Task creation | Pull-down gesture | Form / natural language input |
| Habit tracking | Built-in | Via recurring tasks only |
| Streak logic | Total daily reps | Consecutive days |
| Projects / sub-tasks | No | Yes |
| Team collaboration | No | Yes |
| Calendar integration | No | Yes |
| Data storage | On-device, no account | Cloud, account required |
| Pricing | Free (3 lists) · $6.99 one-time | Free · ~$48/year for Pro |
## Where Dimmly Wins
Dimmly's biggest strength is speed and simplicity. If you've ever opened a task app, gotten overwhelmed by the interface, and closed it without adding anything — Dimmly is designed to solve exactly that. The gesture-based UX removes all the small decisions that slow you down. Its habit features are thoughtfully integrated rather than bolted on, and the one-time price means no subscription guilt and no account to manage. Your data stays private and on your phone.
## Where Todoist Wins
Todoist earns its reputation for a reason. If you're managing complex projects, delegating to teammates, or need your tasks to talk to your calendar, Todoist handles all of it. Its natural language input is genuinely impressive, and cross-platform sync is seamless. For professionals managing high volumes of work across multiple devices and contexts, Todoist is hard to beat.
## Who Should Choose Dimmly?
Dimmly is the right choice if you want a fast, low-friction daily task and habit system that lives on your iPhone. It's well-suited to people who've found apps like Todoist overkill for their actual needs, or who prefer a one-time purchase over a subscription. It is not a project manager or a calendar replacement — and it doesn't try to be.
## Who Should Choose Todoist?
Todoist is the better fit if you work across multiple devices, collaborate with others, or need structured project management with real depth. If your work involves dependencies, delegation, or heavy scheduling, Todoist's ecosystem justifies the subscription cost.
## The Bottom Line
Choose **Dimmly** if you want a beautifully simple iPhone app for daily tasks and habits with no subscription and no clutter. Choose **Todoist** if you need a full productivity platform that scales with complex, collaborative work.
